Progressive Tooling for Serial Production

This project involved the design of a progressive die tool (also known as a stamping mould) for the serial production of a metal bracket made of C45 carbon steel. The process began with analyzing a 2D technical drawing and developing a step-by-step sequence combining punching and bending operations to shape the part.

A strip layout was developed to maximize material efficiency and minimize waste across five manufacturing stages. The tooling system incorporated components such as hardened steel punches, bronze bushings, guide pillars, and springs ‚Äî all carefully dimensioned for alignment and durability under industrial press forces.

The final CAD model of the stamping mould demonstrated the full working interaction between movable and fixed elements. This project strengthened my knowledge in tool engineering and manufacturing processes, while reinforcing essential skills in design interpretation, mechanical analysis, and production-oriented thinking.
